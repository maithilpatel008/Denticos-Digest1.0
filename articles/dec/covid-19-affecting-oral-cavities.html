Covid-19 affecting Oral Cavity
Chitra kesarola
Outbreak of Corona Virus in 2019 rapidly expanded into a worldwide pandemic, creating global health threat and economic crisis. This article throws light on the dental health of patients affected with Covid-19 disease.

INTRODUCTION

Several epidemics such as HINI, H5N1, Ebola, Zika, Nipah etc; have affected worldwide which were successfully handled with appropriate research and efforts.

On 30 th Jan 2020, WHO declared the Chinese outbreak of COVID-19 i.e. 7 th member of Corona Virus family, as Public Health Emergency of International Concern due to it’s rampant spread. It initially originated from Wuhan, China and spread from Horseshoe Bats through droplet spread or contact.

IMPACT OF COVID-19 IN DENTISTRY

Oral Manifestation are developed during the infectious period of the disease. There is growing evidence that ACE2 main host cell receptors of SARS- COV-2 is highly appreciated in epithelial cells of tongue and of salivary glands which explain development of Ageusia , Xerostomia and Ulcerations. Ulcerations are of different types such as Haermorrhagic ulcerations ( seen in upper and lower vermilion border of lip), Apthous like necrotic ulceration ( seen in light lower lips) and more severe and widespread HSV1 necrotic ulcer.

Many opportunistic fungal infections are seen such as Candidiasis and Neurosal Lesions which can also be seen in oral cavity of patients with Covid-19. Some periodontal are seen such as Gingivitis and drugs given to patients such as antiviral (Rhemdesivir), anticoagulants can also lead to oral manifestation.

But there is still doubt about oral manifestation precedding whether SARS COVID-19 infection directly cause oral infection or with COVID-19 progression is yet to be determined.

CONCLUSION

Several changes in oral cavity and disease are manifested due to covid19 . The analyze of changes in oral cavity is a subject of further studies if it is possible or not.
